World Happiness 2015-2019


How Happy are Countries Around the World?

The choropleth graphs below depict the average response for each country to the question, on a scale from 1-10, how happy are you?.
The yellow colours indicate a higher happiness level, whereas a bluer colour represents a lower average response.
Some noticable changes are the sharp decline in happiness for Venezuela and Yemen, who have both been victim to political unrest.


What Factors have Contributed to Happiness Scores?

The radar chart on the left displays some of the criteria that have been used to calculate happiness scores, for the years 2015 and 2019, in pink and blue respectively.
The valuerepresented is the average score for that year, divided by the maximum score for the category in the year 2019. Using this graph, we can see that overall happiness has changed very little, over the 5 year period, although the categories themselves have experienced change. The largest shifts have been an increase in family and economic factors, and a sharp decline in generosity.


Which Countries are the Happiest?

This chart shows which countries take out the top 4 spots for the happiest countries, between 2015 and 2019. We can see that there are only 5, Switzerland, Denmark, Norway, Iceland, and Finland. Looking at the criteria, it shouldn't be surprising that these countries are the happiest, with these countries being known for their outstanding social welfare, gdp per capita, and freedom.


How has Regional Happiness Changed?

The slope graph below depicts the change in overall average happiness for a few of the world's regions, from 2015 to 2019. We can see that the changes have been very, very slight, in accordance with our findings that overall happiness has not changed over this tenure. We can see the upward changes have been with Western Europe, Central and Eastern Europe, and Sub-Saharan Africa. The decreases have occured with North America and Southern Asia.
